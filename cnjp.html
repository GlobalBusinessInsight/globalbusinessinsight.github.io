Even without Sanae Takaichi as Prime Minister, her hawkish specter haunts Japanese politics. The relationship is sliding irreversibly from "Cold Politics, Hot Economics" to a deep freeze in both.

In the corridors of Nagata-cho, a new consensus is hardening. Although the Liberal Democratic Party (LDP) leadership race has settled, the political energy represented by Sanae Takaichi, the standard-bearer of the "hawks," has not dissipated. Instead, it is reshaping Japan's national strategy.

For decades, Sino-Japanese relations were maintained by a delicate implicit agreement: suspicion in security, but deep integration in economics (known as "Cold Politics, Hot Economics").
