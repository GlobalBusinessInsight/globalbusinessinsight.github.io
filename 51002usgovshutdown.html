What is a Government Shutdown?

A U.S. government shutdown occurs when Congress fails to pass, or the President fails to sign, the necessary appropriation bills that fund the federal government. In this situation, the government must suspend "non-essential" services and furlough the associated employees. It is essentially a political deadlock over the budget between the legislative and executive branches.

Federal Employees
