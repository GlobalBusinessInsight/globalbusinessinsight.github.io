[LATEST] With Venezuela's escalated annexation threats towards the Essequibo region in late 2025, and the launch of U.S. "Operation Southern Spear" in the Caribbean, the risk of direct military conflict has reached its highest point in years.

Despite calls from hawks for "decapitation strikes," this report maintains its core assessment: The probability of a full-scale Iraq-style ground invasion remains low (~15%). Washington currently favors a "Naval Blockade" to protect Guyanese oil interests and is reportedly considering outsourcing conflict to Private Military Contractors (PMCs) to mitigate political risk.

However, the recent designation of the Venezuelan gang "Tren de Aragua" as a Foreign Terrorist Organization provides a new legal pretext for surgical strikes under the guise of "counter-terrorism."
